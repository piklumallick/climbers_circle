EXPEDITIONS

Mt. Jogin [2019]

Height: 6,465m (21,211ft)

Location: Garhwal Himalayas, Uttarakhand, India

Mt. Jogin is revered in Hindu mythology, associated with Lord Shiva, and is popular for its serene environment and technical routes. Known for its dramatic rock faces and snow-covered ridges, it provides challenging yet rewarding ascents.

Interesting Fact: Joginâ€™s proximity to the famous Gangotri Glacier makes it a stunning climb, with rare sightings of wildlife like the elusive snow leopard and Himalayan tahr along the route.

Mt. Jupkia [2017]

Height: 5,530m (18,143ft)

Location: Annapurna Range, Nepal

A lesser-known peak, Mt. Jupkia offers solitude and awe-inspiring views of the Annapurna massif. The region is renowned for its unique flora, with forests of rhododendrons and rare alpine plants that bloom spectacularly in spring.

Did You Know? Nepal's Annapurna region is home to over 1,200 species of flowering plants, making it a paradise for nature enthusiasts and botanists.

Friendship Peak [2016]

Height: 5,289m (17,356ft)

Location: Pir Panjal Range, Himachal Pradesh, India

Friendship Peak is ideal for both trekkers and climbers, offering panoramic views of the Pir Panjal and Dhauladhar ranges. The ascent often includes sightings of Himalayan wildlife such as ibex and snow leopards, adding to its allure.

Fun Fact: Friendship Peak provides a relatively accessible mountaineering experience for beginners, especially during the summer climbing season.

Bhagirathi II [2014]

Height: 6,512m (21,365ft)

Location: Garhwal Himalayas, Uttarakhand, India

A technically demanding climb, Bhagirathi II attracts seasoned mountaineers. Known for its imposing granite structure, it overlooks the Bhagirathi River and provides a scenic but challenging experience due to its icy terrain.

Historical Fact: The Bhagirathi peaks are named after the Bhagirathi River, one of the main headstreams of the Ganges, which is highly revered in Hindu mythology.

Mt. Indrasan [2013]

Height: 6,220m (20,407ft)

Location: Kullu Valley, Himachal Pradesh, India

Mt. Indrasan is revered as the seat of Lord Indra, the Hindu god of rain. Its steep ascents are suited for experienced climbers, rewarding them with captivating views of the Dhauladhar and Pir Panjal ranges.

Mythological Insight: According to legend, Indrasan is the abode of Lord Indra, making it a spiritually significant climb for many pilgrims and mountaineers alike.

Mt. Gangstang [2011]

Height: 6,164m (20,217ft)

Location: Lahaul Valley, Himachal Pradesh, India

Mt. Gangstang offers climbers a rugged yet beautiful route in the remote Lahaul region. Known for challenging rock and ice sections, the peak provides panoramic views of the Himalayan ranges and serene landscapes.

Did You Know? The Lahaul Valley is one of the coldest inhabited regions in India, often referred to as "Mini Tibet" due to its terrain and Buddhist culture.

Deo Tibba Peak [2010]

Height: 6,001m (19,688ft)

Location: Pir Panjal Range, Himachal Pradesh, India

Deo Tibba, popular among trekkers, features lush trails that span through alpine meadows. Local legend connects it to Hindu deities, making it a spiritually significant and visually stunning journey.

Cultural Insight: According to local legends, Deo Tibba is the gathering place of the gods, and many locals believe that the deities reside on its summit.

Mt. Shiva [2009]

Height: 6,142m (20,144ft)

Location: Garhwal Himalayas, Uttarakhand, India

Mt. Shiva is often seen as sacred due to its name and proximity to other mythological peaks. Known for stunning glaciers and a diverse ecosystem, it draws both pilgrims and mountaineers seeking challenging climbs.

Interesting Fact: Mt. Shiva is part of the sacred Himalayan ranges, which are often considered the "Abode of the Gods" in Hindu mythology.

Manirang [2008]

Height: 6,593m (21,631ft)

Location: Kinnaur Himalayas, Himachal Pradesh, India

Manirang, a peak of historical significance, once marked the trade route between Spiti and Kinnaur. It presents a challenging ascent with steep rock and ice sections and is surrounded by breathtaking landscapes of the Trans-Himalayan region.

Historical Insight: Traders from Tibet and India used Manirang as a trade pass, exchanging goods such as wool, salt, and barley.

Phawararang [2007]

Height: 6,319m (20,735ft)

Location: Upper Mustang, Nepal

Phawararang is a remote peak offering pristine landscapes in the culturally rich Upper Mustang region. Known for its isolation and rare flora, the trek to Phawararang is as spiritually fulfilling as it is adventurous.

Cultural Note: The Upper Mustang region is known for its Tibetan culture, and the isolated location of Phawararang attracts trekkers seeking a serene and spiritually uplifting experience.

Sanakdenk [2005]

Height: 6,109m (20,033ft)

Location: Upper Mustang, Nepal

Sanakdenk is a remote peak that demands technical skill and endurance. Offering rugged, untouched trails, it provides climbers with panoramic views and a sense of adventure in one of Nepal's most secluded areas.

Insider Tip: Climbers often recommend visiting during the spring or autumn months to enjoy clearer weather and stunning views of the landscapes.

Height: 4,200m (13,780ft)

Location: Kullu Valley, Himachal Pradesh, India

Mythological Significance: It is believed that sage Bhrigu meditated on this mountain, which adds to its historical and spiritual importance among locals.
