TREKS

Lamkhaga Pass Trek - 2019

Located in the Garhwal Himalayas, the Lamkhaga Pass Trek is one of the most challenging treks in the region, spanning 8 days and taking you through the pristine landscapes of the Himalayas. The trek offers breathtaking views of Kinnaur and Garhwal ranges and is known for its remote and rugged trails.

The trek begins from the village of Harsil in Uttarakhand and crosses over to Chitkul, the last village of Himachal Pradesh. Lamkhaga Pass, standing at an impressive altitude of 5,282 meters, is often snow-covered and can be quite challenging.

Fun Fact: Harsil, the starting point, is famous for its apples and is also known as the "Mini Switzerland of India" due to its natural beauty.

Kedarkantha Trek - 2018

The Kedarkantha Trek is a popular 6-day winter trek, celebrated for its snow-covered trails and panoramic views of the Himalayan peaks. This trek is ideal for beginners and offers a mix of dense pine forests, wide clearings, and scenic campsites.

The trek starts from Sankri village and culminates at the Kedarkantha Summit, located at an altitude of 3,810 meters. From the summit, you get stunning views of Swargarohini, Bandarpoonch, and Black Peak.

Interesting Fact: According to local legends, Kedarkantha was initially intended to be the site for Lord Shiva’s Kedarnath temple, but the plans were moved to the current Kedarnath location due to disturbances by villagers.

Markha Valley Trek - 2017

The Markha Valley Trek is a 7-8 day journey in the Ladakh region, known for its stark landscapes and Buddhist monasteries. This trek is a great way to experience the unique culture of Ladakh and the rugged beauty of its valleys.

Starting from Spituk, the trail passes through remote villages, high passes, and crosses the Markha River multiple times, with views of Kang Yatse peak and the Zanskar range. The highest point, Kongmaru La, is at an altitude of 5,200 meters.

Historical Fact: The Markha Valley was part of ancient trade routes and is dotted with Buddhist monasteries, some of which are hundreds of years old.

Rupin Pass Trek - 2017

The Rupin Pass Trek is a thrilling 8-day trek that takes adventurers from Uttarakhand to Himachal Pradesh, featuring diverse landscapes like hanging villages, snow bridges, and dramatic waterfalls. This high-altitude trek is suited for experienced trekkers due to its steep ascents.

Starting from Dhaula, the trek leads to the Rupin Pass at 4,650 meters, with panoramic views of the Dhauladhar range and Kinner Kailash.

Did You Know? The Rupin Pass Trek includes a famous “U-shaped” glacial valley and the Rupin Waterfall, which dramatically descends in multiple tiers.

Parang La Trek - 2015

The Parang La Trek is a challenging 10-day trek that connects the Spiti Valley in Himachal Pradesh to the Ladakh region. Known for its stark desert landscapes, it takes trekkers through high-altitude terrains, glaciers, and river crossings, offering an isolated and remote trekking experience.

Starting from Kibber, one of the world’s highest inhabited villages, the trek crosses Parang La at 5,580 meters before descending to the turquoise Tso Moriri Lake in Ladakh.

Insightful Fact: The Parang La route follows an ancient trade path historically used by traders between Spiti and Ladakh, making it a culturally rich journey.
